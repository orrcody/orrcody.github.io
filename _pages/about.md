I am a labor economist at the U.S. Census Bureau's Center for Economic Studies, where I lead the development of the Post-Secondary Employment Outcomes (PSEO) experimental data product. My work focuses on managing the production of existing statistics, developing new statistics, and conducting research to enhance the utility of the PSEO data. More generally, I am interested in the interaction between higher education and the workforce.

Before joining CES, I earned my PhD in Economics from Michigan State University. In my personal time, I enjoy playing guitar, reading music history, and attending concerts. 

This is a personal website. Any content presented is my own and does not represent the views of the U.S. Census Bureau.
